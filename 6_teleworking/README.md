This step assigns a telework probability and a binary telework flag to each working agent. `6_1_teleworking.ipynb` uses two Statbel sources — the Work/Labour Force Survey 2023 (national counts of telework frequency by NACE sector) and the yearly teleworking survey 2023 (rates by gender and education level) — to compute for each agent a `p_telework_today`: the probability of teleworking on a given day. Frequency categories (always/usually/sometimes/never) are converted to day-equivalents (5/3/1.5/0 days per week), a Brussels boost is applied to correct for the BCR being above the national average, and an education multiplier is applied based on the agent's ISCED level. A binary `telework_today` flag is then drawn via a binomial sample. Agents with `telework_today = 1` are excluded from the commuting population. `6_2_validation.ipynb` checks the resulting telework rates by industry, sex, professional status, education, and age group against the reference distributions.
